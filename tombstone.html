Photobook: This is not a Tombstone

Photographic Project 2 (constructing)

This is not a Tombstone (2012-2017)

Pierrot Bourdieu once described the experience of looking into family albums, claiming that they are tombstones that stand for a dead past.This photographic project tries to challenge his idea as in a digital context, the materiality of photographic practices has shifted sharply from static and chemical mediums (e.g. paper, film, albums) to electrical and digital signals stored in hard drives and spread across the Internet. Screens have made it possible for digital photos to travel in a form of flow, which dramatically overthrows its traditional functions as Bourdieu proposed in the 1950s and 1960s. Instead, it now functions as a part of the phantasmagoria of the modern society.
